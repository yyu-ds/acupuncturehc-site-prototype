Meet the Team

Our licensed practitioners combine rigorous academic training with deep clinical experience in Traditional Chinese Medicine.

Dr. Huan Ma

TCMD, PhD, L.Ac

Dr. Huan Ma comes from a long family line of Traditional Chinese Medicine doctors. He completed his Ph.D in acupuncture and internal medicine at Tianjin University of TCM, served as supervising physician at the Acupuncture and Chinese Affiliated Outpatient Clinic of Tianjin University in China, and was a contributing author to Integration of TCM and Western Medicine Emergency and Practical Internal Medicine. He is a Diplomate of Acupuncture certified by NCCAOM, licensed by the Minnesota Board of Medical Practice, and has served as Associate Dean of Acupuncture Clinical Services and full-time professor at Northwestern Health Sciences University. Dr. Ma is renowned for pain management and difficult, complex medical cases.

Tao Gong

TCMD, L.Ac

Tao Gong completed her bachelor's and Master's of acupuncture at Tianjin University of TCM. She was a staff physician at the Tianjin Internal Medicine Clinic and is currently serving as a clinical supervisor and assistant professor at Northwestern Health Sciences University in Bloomington, MN. Dr. Gong has extensive clinical experience in gynecological issues and cosmetic acupuncture. She is a Diplomate of Acupuncture certified by NCCAOM and licensed by the Minnesota Board of Medical Practice.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about acupuncture, first visits, safety, results, and the services available at Acupuncture Health Center.

Why acupuncture?

Acupuncture, a completely safe and natural medicine, is a form of stimulation therapy, which entices the body into correcting itself. Acupuncture is the oldest continuously practiced form of medicine in the world, with a history spanning 2,500 years and recorded in over 30,000 books. In essence, it is the largest, longest running clinical trial in world history.

Does acupuncture hurt?

The needles used in acupuncture are extremely thin, causing minimal to no pain or discomfort.

What will my first appointment be like?

At your first visit, you will sit down with the acupuncturist to discuss your symptoms or condition. Then you will be asked to lie down on a massage-style table, and the acupuncturist will begin treatment, inserting hair-thin, sterile needles into specific acupuncture points. These needles remain in place for twenty to thirty minutes, after which most people feel very calm and relaxed.

Is acupuncture safe?

Yes, it is very safe. Our practitioners are trained, licensed and certified professionals. Recent studies showed serious side effects in less than 1.1 patients out of 10,000. Because acupuncture is natural medicine, side effects are minimal, and the benefits are significant.

How soon will I see results?

Many people experience immediate relief, while others may need additional treatments before they begin to see results. Every person responds differently to acupuncture, and each ailment takes a different amount of time to treat.
